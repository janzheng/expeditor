# Gut-brain axis in treatment-resistant depression

## goal
Generate novel hypothesis angles for how gut microbiome composition
influences treatment-resistant depression (TRD), with emphasis on
mechanisms that could lead to actionable clinical interventions.

## background
- ~30% of MDD patients don't respond to 2+ antidepressant trials (TRD)
- Gut-brain axis communication via vagus nerve, immune signaling, and microbial metabolites is established
- Recent work links specific taxa (Faecalibacterium, Coprococcus) to depression outcomes
- Gap: most studies are correlational; causal mechanisms and intervention targets are unclear

## agents

### metabolite-lens
Search PubMed for recent work on short-chain fatty acids (especially butyrate),
tryptophan metabolism, and their effects on neuroinflammation in depression.
Focus on: what specific metabolic pathways are disrupted in TRD vs treatment-responsive
depression? Are there metabolites that predict antidepressant non-response?

### immune-lens
Search PubMed for the intersection of gut permeability ("leaky gut"),
systemic inflammation (IL-6, TNF-alpha, CRP), and antidepressant resistance.
Focus on: does gut-driven immune activation create a neuroinflammatory state
that blocks SSRI/SNRI efficacy? What immune biomarkers distinguish TRD?

### vagal-lens
Search PubMed for vagus nerve signaling as a mediator between gut microbiome
and mood regulation. Focus on: vagal tone differences in TRD patients,
whether vagus nerve stimulation outcomes correlate with gut composition,
and microbial species that modulate vagal afferent signaling.

### intervention-lens
Search PubMed for clinical trials of microbiome-targeted interventions
(FMT, psychobiotics, prebiotics, dietary interventions) in depression,
especially TRD. Focus on: what worked, what didn't, what were the effect
sizes, and what gaps remain in the intervention landscape?

## synthesize
Read all agent outputs. Identify:
1. Convergence points — where 2+ lenses point to the same mechanism or target
2. Gaps — mechanisms discussed in one lens that the others missed
3. Top 3 novel hypotheses ranked by: novelty, testability, and therapeutic potential
4. For each hypothesis: what experiment would test it? (study design sketch)

## formatting
For every paper cited, include:
- Full author list (first 3 + et al.), year, title, journal
- PMID or DOI link
- Inline citations as (Author et al., Year) throughout the text

## output
Write findings to `.expo/output/gut-brain-trd-hypotheses.md` with:
- Executive summary (1 paragraph)
- Per-lens findings (key papers, mechanisms identified)
- Synthesis: convergence map, gaps, ranked hypotheses
- Next steps: proposed study designs, potential collaborators/labs to look at
- References section at the end with full citations
